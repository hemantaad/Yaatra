About Yaatra

How it all Started ?

2015- Hemanta Adhikari, an 20-year-old college dropout, seemed to harbour dreams of entrepreneurship which compelled him to take the plunge with his first start-up Sangam. Sangam enabled booking tickets online of different means of transporatation in one place , later on, got converted to become Yaatra.

2017- Adhikari was also selected for Thiel Fellowship, a 2-year-old program started by the founder of C.G group which gave him funding of $100,000 to pursue and make real the idea of his start-up. Yaatra was launched in Kathmandu.

2018- In August of this year, Yaatra received its first Series A funding.

Becoming the Face of Nepali Start-ups

2018- In December of this year, Yaatra received funding of a whopping amount of $25 million from its investors namely Ncell , C.G group and others.

April 2018- Yaatra app was launched. The mobile app was a catalyst in writing the success story of this ticket booking chain start-up that was to follow. It is a user-friendly app that helps customers to book tickets directly. Yaatra, by now had expanded to Pokhara, Dharan, Chitwan, Kakarvitta, Dang, Mahendranagar and was spreading at a massive pace. It has almost come up to 2000 Tourist Buses, 20,000 micros and 100 flights in Nepal

July 2019- Yaatra bagged $100 million in Series C Round of funding from Japanese investor SoftBank. This year proved to be a financially fulfilling year. Yaatra now had the backing of one of the most prominent and powerful investors in the world.

Conquering the Asian Territory and Stepping into the International Markets

2020- This is the year that marked the establishment of the Yaatra empire in Asia-Pacific and kick-started international proliferation by establishing operations in UK, UAE, Dubai, China, Singapore and Indonesia. It also became a unicorn in September when it raised funding of $800 million from SoftBank and already had raised $200 million from its existing investors.

2020- With the backing of Greenoaks, C.G group, Ncell, Hero enterprise and China Lodging Group as its solid group of investors, Yaatra is not far from becoming a global success story, perhaps to some extent it has.
